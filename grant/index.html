03 · The system already exists

A role-based AI organization, not a chatbot.

Le-AI-Lab is governed like a research lab: doctrine, decision rights, RFCs, ADRs, operating cadence, risk register, and a human PI as final authority.

Organization

Orchestrator, builders, adversaries, cold readers

Mode A staffing uses Claude Opus as orchestrator, GPT builders, DeepSeek and Qwen adversaries, cold readers, and probationary hires. The CEO AI recommends; Le Chen remains final authority.

Supply vs demand

Current plan capacity is the constraint

Claude Opus is the production orchestrator and serves as high-value cold skeptic. When current plan capacity is exhausted (roughly mid-week under the system's workload), non-Claude models handle remaining rounds — adequate but not ideal. The ask is to run every round at full Claude quality for the cohort window.

Knowledge

LLM-first graph memory

SPDEs-wiki is an independently designed LLM-first graph knowledge base, inspired by Andrej Karpathy's LLM-wiki concept but built for mathematics: stable citekey slugs, canonical section schemas, typed frontmatter relations such as extends / extended_by, project dependency edges, and a wikilink graph. It remains in access-controlled staging; the public layer is SPDEs-Bib.

LayerStorage and retrieval role
refdbBibTeX text with grep conventions; 8,000+ entries and citekey stability anchoring every emitted reference.
2nd + 3rd brainYAML indices from LaTeX; weighted-field scoring (citekey 10, keywords 8, title 5, theorems 4, abstract 2) over 44 own and 716 curated papers.
SPDEs-wikiMarkdown graph plus derived SQLite FTS5 cache using the unicode61 tokenizer and Python re-ranking; 2,024 pages — 945 papers, 629 people, 270 concepts, 92 notes, 85 projects, 3 theorem pages.

llm_pack717 flattened bundles for direct context ingestion; plain text — LaTeX, YAML, Markdown — is the archival source of truth, while SQLite FTS5 is a rebuildable cache.

Source-grounded corpus. SPDEForge is source-level LaTeX ground truth for all 716 forged papers and books: raw arXiv source to citation-normalized copy unified against the 8,000+-entry refdb to flattened llm_pack with macros expanded. Where no TeX preprint exists — books, pre-ArXiv, journal-only papers — Mathpix or LlamaParse reconstructs OCR-to-LaTeX sources and pushes them through the same normalization, so the grounding layer covers the whole corpus.

Anti-hallucination assumption. LLMs hallucinate; the lab's architecture assumes it. Every citation passes a citekey-grep gate against refdb before emission; every mathematical claim is grounded in llm_pack source or wiki synthesis before use. Three cross-checking KBs — public SPDEs-Bib bibliography, SPDEForge source ground truth, and SPDEs-wiki synthesis — reduce hallucination to a minimum and make residual errors traceable, aligned with Claude Science's reviewer-agent and auditable-artifact philosophy.

05 · Applied reach & societal impact

Surface-growth physics, all the way down.

The SPDEs this project proves theorems about are not abstract: they are the equations of surface growth. Strengthening their proofs raises the reliability of models across the physical and life sciences — the same Broader-Impacts case the PI's active NSF CAREER award already makes.

Recognized at the field's highest honors. Three of science's top prizes sit directly on the objects of this proposal — evidence that this is core physics, not a niche corner of mathematics:

  • Giorgio Parisi — 2021 Nobel Prize in Physics. For discoveries on disorder and fluctuations in physical systems — exactly the KPZ-universality statistics these SPDEs describe.
  • Philip W. Anderson — 1977 Nobel Prize in Physics. For localization in disordered media; the parabolic Anderson model at the heart of this work is named for him, and its localization/intermittency is a physics phenomenon.
  • Martin Hairer — 2014 Fields Medal. For the theory of regularity structures, which finally solved the KPZ equation — the same equation whose analysis-heavy properties this project develops and audits.
The physics

KPZ universality, a Nobel-recognized class

The stochastic heat equation and the KPZ equation describe how randomly deposited matter builds a rough interface — sand-like random vs snow-like ballistic deposition, whose surface fluctuations scale as t1/3 rather than the classical t1/2. This KPZ universality class recurs from crystal growth and flame fronts to bacterial-colony and tumor margins; Giorgio Parisi shared the 2021 Nobel Prize in Physics for the underlying discoveries.

The applications

Manufacturing, environment, public safety

Per the CAREER Broader-Impacts case, accurate surface-growth and interface models matter in semiconductor and pharmaceutical manufacturing, in sediment transport and other environmental challenges, and in forest-fire-front management. Trustworthy, auditable proofs of the governing SPDEs raise the reliability of every such downstream model.

The through-line

An established open-science record

The knowledge commons here is not a first attempt. The CAREER program already ships public open source: the SPDEs-Bib reference bank and the pip-installable tetris-ballistic surface-growth simulator (pip install tetris-ballistic) used for STEM outreach on the KPZ class. This proposal's vetted digests, obstruction maps, and reusable skills extend that track record.

Intellectual merit. The stochastic heat equation, parabolic Anderson model, and stochastic wave equation are canonical SPDEs — playing a role for random surfaces that Brownian motion plays for stochastic differential equations. New, auditable results on their moment asymptotics and rough-noise thresholds advance the frontier of probability and mathematical physics, and deepen understanding of surface-growth phenomena and universality classes.

Why AI amplifies this. Because these proofs are analysis-heavy, they are exactly the un-formalizable regime Lean cannot yet reach — so a verified, adversarially-reviewed AI loop is the difference between fluent-but-unchecked drafts and citable, replayable results. Cohort credits convert directly into trustworthy mathematics on a federally-funded, high-impact research program.

07 · Differentiation

Why this is not Axiom Math.

Axiom Math and Harmonic validate the verification thesis. The differentiator is not autonomy; it is verified autonomy in the adjacent niche they structurally do not cover.

Venture-scale formal-proof companies are building the formalizable future; Le-AI-Lab is building an auditable standard for the analysis-heavy mathematics that formal methods cannot yet reach. The landscape memo notes Brownian motion in Lean only in Nov 2025 and first machine-checked Itô calculus in June 2026; SPDE infrastructure remains absent.
